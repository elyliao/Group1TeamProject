Hepatitis A is an inflammation of the liver that is primarily spread through the ingestion of food or water contaminated with the Hepatitis A Virus (HAV), in which the contamination is often due to contact with fecal matter of an infected person. Unlike Hepatitis B and C, it does not lead to chronic liver disease, but it can still be fatal -- in rare cases, it can eventually lead to acute liver failure, or fulminant hepatitis (1).

Close contact with an infected person.
- Sexual contact
- Caring for an infected person
Consuming contaminated items.
- Food items can be contaminated anytime from the growing process to even after cooking (2).

Other symptoms may include (but are not limited to): diarrhea, loss of appetite, fever, jaundice, and joint pain (3).

Liver -- this is the main area of inflammation. Jaundice can be a symptom of Hepatitis A as it is caused by issues with bilirubin production, which involves the liver (4)

The digestive system/Stomach -- Common symptoms of Hepatitis A include abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting, loss of appetite, and diarrhea. These symptoms are very common among other infections/illnesses.

Joint pain -- joint pain can be caused by many things, including medical issues and old age, however they can also be caused by viral infections such as Hepatitis A (5).
